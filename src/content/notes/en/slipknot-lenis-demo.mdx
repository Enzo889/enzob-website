Creating immersive scroll-driven experiences requires smooth scrolling and precise animation controls. For this recent Slipknot demo, I achieved this by combining **Lenis** and **Motion**, exploring a variety of interactive techniques.

## Architecture

Instead of going with a monolithic approach, I modularized the Astro page. I gave each behavior its own reusable component, keeping the complex scroll logic isolated and perfectly synced. 

## Animations

My demo relies heavily on tracking the `scroll`, `velocity`, `direction`, and `progress` to trigger distinct visual effects.

### Parallax
I built the `ParallaxSection` to push background imagery at an offset speed relative to the page scroll. This generates depth without losing any responsiveness.

### Reveals & Scrambles
For text and content reveals, I used targeted data attributes (like `data-enter="scale"`) coupled with Motion. These attributes trigger stagger scale, slash, or upward reveals. Additionally, I added a `CharScrambleSection` that randomizes text strings before settling into the correct characters exactly when I want them to enter the viewport.

### Marquee & Sticky
I implemented a `VelocityMarqueeSection` that reacts to the scroll speed—the faster you scroll, the faster my marquee runs. Alongside this, the `StickyCounterSection` and `HorizontalScrollSection` pin elements in place and map vertical scroll distance to the horizontal timeline progress.

### Structure Example

```astro
<ParallaxSection
  id="s-parallax"
  imgId="para-1"
  imgSrc={img_live1.src}
  alt="Slipknot Live"
>
  <span class="section-tag" data-enter="scale">lenis.on('scroll')</span>
  <h2 class="section-title reveal-heading" data-enter="slash">Driven by scroll</h2>
</ParallaxSection>
```

By leveraging these specific components, I ensured that every scroll interaction feels alive.
